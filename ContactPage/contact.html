Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, you need a fishing license for most fishing activities in Massachusetts. For freshwater fishing, you need a freshwater fishing license. For saltwater fishing, you need a saltwater fishing permit. You can purchase these online through the Massachusetts Division of Fisheries and Wildlife website or at authorized retailers.

Boston offers year-round fishing opportunities, but the best times are typically spring and fall. Spring (April-June) is excellent for striped bass as they migrate into the area. Summer (July-August) offers good fishing for bluefish and fluke. Fall (September-November) sees another run of striped bass and bluefish as they migrate south.

Yes! Our team of experienced local anglers offers guided fishing trips for both beginners and experienced fishermen. We can customize trips based on the species you're targeting, your experience level, and preferences. Contact us through the form above to inquire about availability and rates.

The equipment you need depends on where and what you're fishing for. For saltwater fishing, a medium-heavy rod and reel combination with 15-20 lb test line is versatile. For freshwater, a lighter setup with 6-10 lb test is suitable. Essential gear includes hooks, sinkers, bobbers, pliers, and a tackle box. Don't forget safety equipment like sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water.
